Chocolate Chip:

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 Cup of brown sugar
  • 1 Cup of softened butter
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Teaspoon of salt
  • 1 Teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 Teaspoons of hot water
  • 2 Teaspoons of vanille extract
  • 3 Cups of all purpose flour
  • 2 Cups if chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Cream the softend butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar together until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time.
  4. Add the vanilla and salt.
  5. Dissovle baking soda in hot water and add to the mixture.
  6. Mix in the flour and then add the chocolate chips.
  7. Roll the dough into a ball and place on cooking sheet 1 inch apart.
  8. Bake for 10 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.

Orange Snowballs:

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup of softend butter
  • 3/4 cup of sifted powder sugar
  • 1 Teaspoon of finely shredded orange peel and set that aside
  • 2 Teaspoons of finley shredded orange peel and set aside
  • 1 Tablespoon of orange juice
  • 2 2/3 Cups of all purpose flour
  • Granulated sugar that is for later
  • 3/4 Cup of granulated sugar

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees
  2. In a larger mixing bowl beatthe butter and turn the mixer on to medium or high speed for 30 seconds.
  3. Add the powders sugar and beat until fully combined, scraping the sides of the bowl occasionally.
  4. Beat in the orange juice until combined.
  5. Beat in as much flour as you can with the mixer, stir the rest of the flour and add 1 teaspoon of orange peel with a wooden spoon.
  6. Shape the dough into 1/4 inch balls and roll them into granulated sugar that you set aside.
  7. Place each ball 2 inches apart onungreassed cooking sheet.
  8. Bake for 15 minutes or until the bottms are lightly browned, let cool for 5 mintues.
  9. While cookies are baking, in a food processor or blender combine the 3/4 cup of sugar and 2 teaspoons of orange peel.
  10. Roll the warm cookies into the mixer and put them back on the sheet to cool completly.

  • 3/4 Cups of sugar
  • 1 Large egg
  • 1 Teaspoon of vanilla

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees and line to cooking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl combine th eflour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In mixer bowl, using the paddle attachment, combine the butter and sugar. Beat on medium speed until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the egg and vanilla and beat for i minute until fully incorporated.
  5. On low speed, add the dry ingredients until just combined and lit it chill for 1 hour.
  6. Roll the dough into 1 inch balls and place on a cooking sheet 3 inches apart.
  7. Add a thumb print to the cookie on the sheet and place in the oven for 15 minutes or until golden brown.

Snickerdoodles:

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup of unsalted butter, softened at room tempature

  • 3 Teaspoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 Teaspoons of cream of tartar
  • 1 Teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 1/2 Teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of salt

Topping:

  • 1/4 Cup of sugar
  • 1 Teaspoon of cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees and line to cooking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Make the topping mixture by mixing the two ingredients together in a smal bowl until fully incorporated and set it to the side.
  3. Using a mixer with a paddle attachment, cream the butter for 1 mintue until smooth.
  4. Add the sugar at a medium speed until light in color.
  5. Mix in the egg and vanilla.
  6. In a seprate bowl mix in all the dry ingredients and slowly add the mixture to the mixing bowl.
  7. Roll them dough into 1/2 - 2 inch balls.
  8. Rolling the ball into the topping mixture and place on a cooking sheet.
  9. Bake for 11 mintues.
  10. While the cookies are still warm flatten them with the back of a spoon and allow them to cool.

Peanutbutter:

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 Cup of softened butter
  • 1/2 Cup of peanut butter
  • 1/2 Cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 Cup of packed brown sugar /or 1/4 cup of honey
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of vanilla
  • 1 1/4 Cups of all-purpose flour
  • Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 Cup of semisweet chocolate pieces(optional)
  • 1 Teaspoon of shortening(optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees and line to cooking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl beat the butter and peanut butter with an electric mixer on medium to high speed for 30 seconds.
  3. Add 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, brown sugar, baking soda, and baking powder. Beat until combined, scraping the sides of the bowl occasionally.
  4. Beat in the egg and vanilla until combined.
  5. Beat in as much flour as you can with the mixer. Stir any remaining flour with a wooden spoon.
  6. If necessary, cover and chill the dough for about 1 hour or until easy to handle.
  7. Shape the dough into 1 inch balls.
  8. Roll in granulated sugar to coat and then place balls 2 inches apart on a cooking sheet.
  9. Flatten by making crisscross marks with the tine of a fork.
  10. Bake for 7 to 9 minutes or until the bottoms are lightly browned.
  11. Transfer cookies to a wire rack and let cool.
  12. If desired, a small saucepan melt the chocolate pieces and shortening over low heat, stirring constantly.
  13. Drizzle the chocolate mixture over cookies; let stand until set.

Frosted Butterscotch:

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 Cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 Teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of salt
  • 1 1/2 Cups of packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 Cup of shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 8-once carton of dairy sour cream
  • Walnuts halves(optional)

Frosting:

  • 1/2 Cup of butter
  • 3 1/2 Cups of sifted powdered sugar
  • 5 Teaspoons of boiling water
  • 1 1/2 Teaspoons of vanilla

Directions for Frosting:

  1. In a medium heavy saucepan heat and stir 1/2 cup butter over medium-low heat until golden brown(do not scroch).
  2. Remove from the heat.
  3. Stir in the 3 1/2 cups of sifted powdered sugar, 5 teaspoons of boiling water, and 1 1/2 teaspoons of vanilla.
  4. Beat until smooth, spreadable frosting.
  5. Immediately frost the cookies(If the frosting begins to get stiff, add a small amount of boiling water and stir until smooth).

Directions for Cookies:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees and line to cooking sheets with parchment paper.
  3. In a bowl mix together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt(set aside).
  4. In a large mixing bowl beat brown sugar and shortening with an electric mixer on medium to high speed for 30 seconds.
  5. Add the eggs and the vanilla.
  6. Beat until fully combined and then scrap the sides of the bowl occasionally.
  7. Alternately add the flour mixture and sour cream to egg mixture, beating well after each addition.
  8. Stir in chopped walnuts with wooden spoon.
  9. Drop dough by rounded teaspoons 2 inches apart onto cooking sheet.
  10. Bake for 10 to 12 mintues or until edges are lightly browned.
  11. Tranfer cookies to wire rack and let them cool.
  12. Frost the cookies with the frosting you made.

Iced Sugar Cookies:

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup of unsalted butter at room tempature
  • 1 Cup of granulated sugar
  • 3 Cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 Teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 Large egg
  • 1 Tablespoon of vanilla extract or vanilla bean paste
  • 1/4 Teaspoon of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to to 375 degrees.
  2. Mix dry ingredients in a medium bowl.
  3. Mix the egg and vanilla together.
  4. In a mixer cream the butter and sugar for 5 minutes.
  5. Beat in the egg and vanilla mixture in little protions.
  6. Add 1/3 cup of flour into the mixture beating at a low speed until fully incorporated. Do the same for the rest of the dry mixture.
  7. Empty into a bowl and kneed the dough until it comes together.
  8. Warp and chill the dough for 4-8 mintues.
  9. Roll the dough between two partchment papers until desired thickness and run a spatulat underneath when done.
  10. Cut out the shapes you would like
  11. Place cut ous on cooking sheets lined with partchment paper and bake for 4 mintues and then turn the try.
  12. Let bake for another 4 munites.
  13. Let cool for 15 mintues.

Directions:

  1. I a mixer place water and meringue powder, mix for 1 to 2 mintues.
